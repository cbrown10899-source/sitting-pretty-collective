Wildflowers on the Parkway

📍 Blue Ridge Parkway · Virginia
Field conditions there right now checking the skies…
Miles walked: 4½ — the fence line, twice
❦ ❦ ❦

Nobody plants the best gardens in Virginia. They plant themselves, along four hundred miles of fence line.

We left before coffee, which is how you know it was her idea. The fog was still deciding what to do with itself when we pulled onto the Parkway, and by the first overlook it had settled low into the valley, leaving the fence lines to us.

That's where the flowers were — not in anybody's garden, but shoulder to shoulder along the split rails: black-eyed Susans, Queen Anne's lace, and a blue chicory that refused to be photographed the same color twice.

We walked one stretch of fence for the better part of an hour and drove away with two hundred photographs and no regrets. A man on a motorcycle stopped to ask if we'd lost something. In a way, we told him, that was the point.
