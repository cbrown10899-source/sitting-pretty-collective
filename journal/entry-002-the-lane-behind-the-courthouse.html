The Lane Behind the Courthouse

📍 Historic District · Small Town, Virginia
Field conditions there right now checking the skies…
Miles walked: 2¼ — mostly cobblestone
❦ ❦ ❦

Every good town keeps one street it forgot to modernize. You find it by following the brick.

We found it the usual way: parked badly, walked the wrong direction, and ended up exactly where we should have been. Behind the courthouse the asphalt gives up and the brick takes over, and with it comes a barber pole, a bookshop that smells like rain, and a general store that has apparently been "closing soon" since 1987.

The store is the kind of place with a ladder on rails and a proprietor who calls the stock "inventory" ironically. We asked what was oldest in the shop. He pointed at himself, then sold us a market basket like his grandmother used to weave — and two jars of honey from a farm four miles up the road.
